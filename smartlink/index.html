SmartLink

Power up your skills. Build the smart homes of tomorrow.

  • Role: Programmer
  • Genre: 2D Serious Game (Simulation & Puzzle)
  • Engine: Unity Engine
  • Platform: PC, Mobile
  • Duration: 4 months

SmartLink is a 2D pixel art serious game developed in collaboration with the Handwerkskammer Ulm, designed to introduce students aged 14-18 to the fundamentals of electrical engineering.

Players take on the role of an apprentice electrician, tasked with installing and repairing devices before integrating them into a smart home system. The game combines realistic simulation tasks such as cable laying, device installation, and system troubleshooting with logic-based puzzles that challenge players to connect smart home components through programmable logic.

SmartLink offers an authentic glimpse into the workflows of modern electricians, blending educational content with interactive gameplay to spark curiosity and engagement in technical professions.

Please note that all screenshots and videos reflect the work of the entire team!

Free Flow Logic Levels

I was responsible for implementing the Free Flow logic levels into SmartLink. In these levels, players connect installed smart home devices by dragging virtual cables between logic blocks. This system challenges players to plan wiring paths carefully, mirroring real-world smart home installations.

Point-and-Click Gameplay Sequences

I developed the gameplay sequences for the initial levels of SmartLink, introducing players to essential electrical engineering tasks through interactive point-and-click mechanics.

In the first level, players learn how to install and connect devices like cameras, sensors, switches, and lamps. Mechanics include cable routing, slot cutting, and device installation using a drag-and-drop system with intuitive visual and auditory feedback.

In the second level, players troubleshoot and repair existing devices inside a smart home environment. Tasks include diagnosing faults with a multimeter, replacing defective components, and soldering new parts into place. This level balances technical realism with accessible gameplay to keep students engaged while learning complex concepts.

I additionally implemented the inventory system used across all levels of SmartLink. The system allows players to drag and drop items to interact with devices. Beyond that, I supported smaller gameplay features, such as the cable reparing sequence in Level 3.
